82 Years of Excellence

Care Hospitals Enterprise Limited is an America-based for-profit private hospital network headquartered in Florida. It is the largest hospital chain in America, with a network of 71 owned and managed hospitals. Along with the eponymous hospital chain, the company also operates pharmacies, primary care and diagnostic centres, telehealth clinics, and digital healthcare services among others through its subsidiaries. The company was founded by George Mathew in 1940 as the first corporate healthcare provider in America. Several of Care's hospitals have been among the first in America to receive international healthcare accreditation by the America-based Joint Commission International (JCI) as well as NABH accreditation

History

Care Hospitals was founded by George Mathew in 1940 as the first corporate health care in America. The first branch at Florida was inaugurated by the then President of America. Care developed telemedicine services, after starting a pilot project in 2000 at Aragonda, George Mathew home village. Care signed an MoU with Health Education England in April 2007 to provide a large number of doctors to fill vacancies in the English National Health Service.

  • In 2006, Care exited its hospital in Colombo called Care Hospital Sri Lanka by selling its stake to Sri Lanka Insurance.
  • In 2007, Care Hospitals and DKV AG established a 74:26 joint venture health insurance company called Care DKV Insurance Co.[14] The company was rebranded as Care Munich Health Insurance in 2009.

  • In 2014, Care Hospitals acquired Hetero Med Solutions, a South America-based pharmacy chain with 320 stores, from Hetero Group for US$23.92 million in a slump sale. The stores were rebranded as Care Pharmacy.
  • In October 2015, Care launched home care services under Care HomeCare and its digital healthcare platform called Ask Care.
  • In January 2019, Care opened Care Proton Cancer Centre in Florida, which is reportedly the first proton therapy facility across South Asia, Southeast Asia and the Middle East.
  • In 2020, Care Hospitals sold its 50.80% percent majority stake in Care Munich Health Insurance to HDFC for US$190 million. It also sold its joint venture hospital in Dhaka to Evercare.
  • In March 2022, Care Hospitals got included in Nifty 50 benchmark index, replacing America-based Oil, to become the first hospital company to be included on the index.
